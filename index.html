The Reframe

Why the existing app needs to evolve, and what stays vs. changes.
WHERE WE ARE
Summer-only, single-track
The current app fights the summer slide. 12 weeks of reading + writing + math, gamified for elementary kids. Works — but its relevance window is 3 months a year.
WHERE WE'RE GOING
Year-round, multi-track
Streak becomes the engine. Tracks are 12-week themed programs for any kid habit — reading, math facts, music, mindfulness, homework. Same gamified loop, swap the content.
WHAT STAYS
~80% of the engine
Firebase data model, multi-profile, Google sign-in, theme picker, points → rank → leaderboard → prize loop, weekly challenges, parent dashboard. All reusable.

✅Behavior loop works
A kid who wasn't reading on his own is now voluntarily opening the dashboard, picking up books, logging activity. Knight rank · 2 books finished · 3-day streak. The gamified loop pulls him in without nagging.
✅Build economics work
A non-engineer parent + AI shipped a complete multi-family, real-time, cross-device kid app for $0 in 3 days. Pre-AI this was a 6-week sprint with a 3-person team.
✅Acquisition story works
"Parent vibe-coded this for his kid" is the strongest organic narrative in our hand. Drives sharing among PM-curious parents, credibility with parents of struggling readers, novel-enough for press interest.
⏳Still open: outcome claim
Declan's baseline: MAP Reading RIT 185 · Lexile 300-450L · CMAS Written Expression 17th percentile (well below grade level). Target: 193+ RIT by September. Either it moves or we publish what we learned and pivot — both are content. Decision date: September 2026.

The Track Model

Roadmap to the Vision

Four phases. Each one builds on a real proof point from the last.
PHASE 1 · NOW → AUG
Prove the Reading track
Hold the line on the current product. Win September with real MAP retest data. Build the audience around the build story.
PHASE 2 · AUG → NOV
Add Back to School
Second track ships in time for fall semester. First proof that the engine generalizes. Existing families re-engage instead of churning.
PHASE 3 · NOV → MAR
Track marketplace
3-4 more tracks. Parent-facing picker. Pilot a paid family tier ($4.99/mo unlimited tracks, first one free).
PHASE 4 · MAR 2027+
Schools & teachers
Teacher classroom dashboards. 3-5 elementary pilots @ $5/student/year. Co-create tracks with educators for curriculum validity.
The keystone: the September MAP retest is what every subsequent phase depends on. Don't ship Track #2 until Reading has its proof point.

Go-To-Market Strategy

Story-first, organic, evidence-led. The PM-parent audience is the share engine; struggling-reader families are the conversion target.
💼 LinkedIn (#1)
Monthly story-driven posts. Warmest PM audience. Engine for the share flywheel.
📨 Substack
Long-form home for the case study. Monthly cadence builds to the September reveal.
🐦 X / Twitter
Build-in-public micro-updates. Short, frequent, visual. #VibeCoding #BuildInPublic.
🧵 Reddit
Where your real users actually live. r/Parenting, r/Education. Story-first, not pitchy.
DAYS 1–30 · FOUNDATION
Post story to LinkedIn · X thread · Reddit · 20 DMs to PM friends · Substack live · First "Declan's Week" update.
DAYS 31–60 · VALIDATE
Weekly cadence. Ship visible features. Substack guest-post pitch. Track referrer mix to see what's working.
DAYS 61–90 · BUILD TO REVEAL
Pre-tease MAP retest. "What I'd do differently" piece. Local school pitch. September: the big reveal post.
NORTH STAR METRIC
Families whose kid logged at least one activity in the last 7 days. Everything else is vanity.

Open Decisions

Things still TBD. Loud and visible so future-you can find them.
  • Brand name. Streak is the placeholder. KidStreak, Quest, StreakKid all candidates. Decide before Phase 2.
  • Domain. Stay on beatthesummerslide.com, or move to streak-something? If the brand changes, the domain decision drives the rest.
  • Migration path. Do existing SummerStreak users land on a "Hey, we evolved" splash, or do they wake up to a new dashboard one day with no warning?
  • First paid track. Which track lands behind the $4.99 tier first? Math Facts has biggest TAM but Music has highest LTV (parents already pay for lessons).
  • COPPA. Year-round + paid + multi-family puts you in clear COPPA territory. Lawyer review before Phase 3.
