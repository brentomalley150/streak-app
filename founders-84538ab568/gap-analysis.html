PRD Gap Analysis

What's missing from the Summer Slide PRD

Benchmarked against CrewCares / BuildtheCrew v2 · Owner: Kate O'Malley · Updated: May 30, 2026

TL;DR

The Summer Slide PRD is a 5-section scaffold of prompts — the headings are right, but every section is a placeholder with no real content. CrewCares v2 is a near-review-ready Stage-4 document with 14 sections.

Beyond empty content, the Summer Slide PRD is missing seven whole sections v2 treats as core: a testable hypothesis, strategic fit / competition, a defensibility (MOAT) argument, a "why agentic" justification, an AI behavior spec with safety guardrails, an eval-driven roadmap, and open questions with working positions. Because Summer Slide targets children, the absent child-safety / COPPA / consent posture is the single most urgent gap.

1 The bar — what CrewCares v2 does well

  • A falsifiable hypothesis with an outcome metric, leading indicators, a validated measurement instrument, and an explicit falsification condition.
  • A problem section with cited evidence — real stats, current workarounds, and the cost of inaction.
  • Three deep personas with jobs-to-be-done, pains, the key product moment, and build-for / cut-for lists.
  • Strategic fit — why-now, a brand wedge, and a named competitive table with each rival's structural gap.
  • A standalone Defensibility / MOAT section with falsifiable watch metrics and an honest "what we don't have yet."
  • A "why agentic, not rule-based" argument that puts judgment in the agent and safety-critical decisions in deterministic rules.
  • An AI behavior spec (20 input→output examples) plus hard rejection / safety criteria.
  • An eval-driven roadmap: riskiest-assumption MVP, quantified go/no-go gates, eval harness, and rollback.
  • A metrics table with baselines, targets, timeframes and guardrails, and open questions paired with working positions.

3 Action items

Prioritized work to bring the PRD up to the v2 bar. Owners are starting assignments — adjust as needed.

P0 · Critical — do first
  • Write the Child-Safety, Privacy & Consent sectionCOPPA (under-13 data + verifiable parental consent), FERPA if selling to schools, parent/teacher account control, data minimization for minors, and safe leaderboard/social design (no exposing kids' names, locations, or contact).Kate
  • Write the AI Behavior Spec + "why agentic" + hard safety rulesDefine what the AI does (level-appropriate recommendations, comprehension check-ins, parent summaries, nudges), justify agent vs. rules, then 15–20 input→output examples and refusals (never recommend age-inappropriate content; never message a child without guardian visibility; never expose one child's data to another).Kate
P1 · Core PRD rigor

  • Add Strategic Fit + competitive tableWhy-now + brand wedge, and a named landscape — Beanstack, Scholastic Summer Reading, Renaissance/Accelerated Reader, library programs, Epic!/Reading Eggs — each with its structural gap and our "why we win."Kate
