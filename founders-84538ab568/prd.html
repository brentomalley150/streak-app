6 Why Agentic, Not Rule-Based

Determinism where fairness, speed, and safety matter; agentic AI where judgment and synthesis matter. The split is the architecture.

Kid game · rules-based

The competition must be deterministic

Points, streaks, ranks, and leaderboards have to be exact and un-gameable (an LLM would be inconsistent and exploitable), run instantly on-device/offline, never expose a child to model free-text, keep a minor's data local (COPPA), and cost nothing per tap.

Parent analysis · agentic

Turning activity into insight is judgment

Synthesizing raw logs into "what happened and what to do" — diagnosing against the hypothesis and recommending the next action — is exactly the judgment work an agent is for.

What makes the parent side genuinely agentic (not just an LLM summary) — a multi-step loop:

  • Retrieve — this week's activity log, the spring baseline (MAP RIT 185, Lexile, writing %), and the research-backed targets.
  • Diagnose — interpret signals against the hypothesis (e.g., "minutes on pace, writing skipped — and writing is the lever for comprehension").
  • Prioritize — surface the one thing that matters this week, not ten metrics.
  • Propose — a concrete next action plus a draft nudge.
  • Confirm-before-action — the parent approves; only then does it write back to the kid app (set the week's focus, add a bonus challenge, schedule a reminder).

AI may still assist the kid side — but only batch, server-side, curated, and content-filtered (book recommendations, comprehension questions, adaptive difficulty). The child never talks to a live model; the rules engine serves pre-computed, safe options.

7 AI Behavior Spec & Guardrails

The parent-facing agent — representative input → expected output.

InputExpected output
6 days logged, minutes high, 0 writing entries"On pace for volume, behind on writing — the lever for the 17%→32% target. Suggested focus: one sentence per session." Plus a proposed nudge.
4-day gap in activitySurfaces the slip gently; proposes a re-engagement nudge and a smaller daily goal. Never shaming.
Parent asks "is he reading hard enough books?"Answers from logged titles/levels vs. his Lexile band; flags if books are too easy or too hard.
Projection drifts below the fall targetExplains why in plain language and the single change that would bend the curve back.
Strong, balanced weekSpecific, genuine encouragement tied to the data; suggests a stretch goal.

Hard guardrails — must hold across every response:

  • Ground every claim in the logged numbers — no hallucinated progress.
  • The fall-score projection is an estimate, never stated as a promise.
  • No clinical or diagnostic inferences (e.g., "dyslexia") — escalate to "worth raising with his teacher."
  • Supportive tone; never shame the child or the parent.
  • A minor's data is consent-gated and never shared across families; confirm-before-action on anything that writes to the kid app.

8 Child Safety, Privacy & Consent

Summer Slide serves children, so safety and consent are a first-class product surface — not a back-office concern. This is the floor everything else is built on.

  • COPPA compliance. Verifiable parental consent before any data is collected from a child under 13; a parent or guardian creates or approves the child's account.
  • FERPA (school channel). When sold through schools or districts, student data is treated as an educational record — the district is the consent authority and data is used only to run the program.
  • Data minimization. Collect the least possible from a minor; default to on-device storage and sync only what the parent analysis needs, with parental consent.
  • Safe social design. Leaderboards show first name + avatar only — never full name, location, school, or contact. Friends are added only by invite link or score code; no open discovery, no stranger contact, no free-text messaging between kids.
  • Parental controls. The parent/guardian owns the account and can view activity, manage prizes and points, reset progress, and gate settings behind a PIN.
  • Age-appropriate content. Any recommended books or generated comprehension questions are filtered for age/grade and reviewed; no unmoderated content reaches a child.
  • No ads, no resale. No behavioral advertising to children and no selling or sharing of a minor's data with third parties. Encryption in transit and at rest, with audit logging on access.
  • Sponsors & prizes. Sponsor-funded prizes are disclosed to parents, never gate participation, and sponsors receive only aggregate, de-identified metrics.

Open items for counsel: the verifiable-consent method, data-retention windows, and the exact COPPA/FERPA posture for the school vs. consumer channels.
